Weekly 2 - All Watched Over By Machines of Loving Grace

By Kristin Wong

How is Ayn Rand's philosophy relevant today?

Ayn Rand’s philosophy of “Objectivism” suggests that humans are meant to live life free from all forms of political control, and to live life for their own selfish desires. In doing so, all humans then become heroes, because we become our own heroes, for we are the only ones that matter. Objectivism was essentially the creation of a new style of morality. While this may seem selfish and against many human’s initial instinct towards altruism, objectivism is still very applicable in our lives in that it enforces the message that we must live for ourselves, for we are in power of whether or not we are happy and satisfied with the way our lives are.

How was the internet shaped by Objectivism?

Objectivism shaped the internet because our society, specifically beginning with a group of entrepreneurs in Silicon Valley, began to shift towards the idea of living life for themselves and free from any political or governmental or “greater” power, and therefore turned to computers to achieve this. It was suggested that new computer technologies could turn everyone into heroes because computer networks could create a new order of control where politics wouldn’t be involved. Although there was a fear that too much power to the individual would eventually lead to anarchy, there was a cybernetic dream where computers and the internet would be used to create a “self-stabilizing system” where we’d be “completely free to follow our own desires.” A great example of this was the early projected computer game called “Pong,” created by Loren Carpenter. The game required the crowd to work as a team to place the paddle where it needed to be in order to keep the ping pong ball in play. Carpenter believed he created his own society where everyone decided where they wanted to be without any guidance, yet “since they were guided by a machine, there was still an order.” This is a very simplified example of how the internet can help humans achieve objectivism because with the internet, though people have the freedom of choice, they can still form “a subconscious consensus.”
